HROpSys 2023

Saturday, 4th November 2023, 10 to 5 p.m.

The 7th event of HROPSYS was organized by the HR, Operations, and Systems Departments of CRKIMR & CIMR on Saturday, 04th of November, 2023. "HROPSYS" is the students' forum of students of HR, Operations, and System specialization of Chetana's Institutes of Management and Research. The forum integrates three functional areas and provides a platform for students to adopt a multi-disciplinary approach.

The HROpSys 2023, WorkRevolution 5.0, Tech-driven Optimisation' aims to gain a deeper understanding of the opportunities and challenges of Industry 5.0 from a multi-stakeholder perspective. Work Revolution 5.0 was an effort to bring different corporate thought leaders to share their perspectives on the possible applications and challenges of the new-age technologies namely Artificial Intelligence, Cobots, Digital Twins, Block Chain, IoT, Big Data Analytics, 6G, and beyond in Industry 5.0.

The event was declared open by Dr. Madhumita Patil, CEO CRKIMR and CIMR. Dignitaries were welcomed by President Shri Shridutt Haldankar and CEO ma’am. Dr. Kavita Khadse, Senior faculty-IT, CRKIMR and Dr. Sandeep Nemlekar, Professor- Operations, CIMR set the context by sharing the genesis of HROpSys and the theme of HROpSys 2023. Dr, Mahesh Luthia, Professor-HR, CIMR and Ms. Lipika Koli, Assistant Professor, HR, CRKIMR introduced the concept and themes of the Ideathon Competition.

Our keynote speakers unveiled how Industry 5.0 technologies can foster collaboration, innovation, and adaptability within organizations, ultimately creating a more efficient, agile, and people-centric work environment centred around sustainability. The keynote speakers comprised of :

● Mr. Vishal Sharma, Global head, Glenmark Pharmaceuticals.
● Mr. Kunal Moon, Associate, Director, BFSI, Gupshup.
● Mr. Vishal Chandratre, Senior Manager, Godrej and Boyce Mg.co.ltd.

Mr. Vishal Sharma shared his insights into how technology is helping mankind to work more effectively and efficiently to manage the given work. He presented an overview on the changing nature of work, the role of technology at workplace. He created awareness among the students on the top 10 skills required in 2025. His findings on the new skills paradigm, encouraged students to reflect on how they must view their careers breaking functional silos. He also highlighted the role of HR as a strategic business partner in the next normal leading organisations and the importance of good data for data-driven decision making.

Mr. Kunal Moon highlighted on the evolution of the Industrial Revolution. He spoke about the technology journey from Industry Revolution 1.0 to 5.0. He also focused on what problem technology is going to solve to make our lives easier. He elaborated with some examples on how technology enables users with diverse experiences. He provided examples on how robotics jave replaced manpower in manufacturing and service industries and how organizations use data to develop their strategies to grow their customer base.

Mr. Vishal Chandratre highlighted the three pillars of digital transformation which included Rejuvenate, Innovate and Pioneer. As part of Rejuvenate he spoke on how technologies such AI, ML, Industry 4.0 can improve processes and bring efficiency. He demonstrated how Smart manufacturing, advanced analytics, enterprise analytics, etc could help organisations innovate. He emphasised the need for organisations to be pioneers in implementing technology. He shared an example of AI and ML implementation in Godrej for defect detection in wielding and color judgment. Under pioneering he talked about omnichannel customer experience and connected products. He also discussed what is next for the industry like smart manufacturing, advanced analytics, etc.

The Session ended with a vote of thanks by Dr. Nandita Mishra, Director, CIMR

‘Envisioning Organisations in Industry 5.0 : A future outlook’

The enriching inaugural session was followed by a panel discussion where industry experts to shared their perspectives on how the future may unfold in Industry 5.0, in terms of potential, challenges and future directions.

The panel members for the panel discussion were as follows:
● Mr. Piyush Malani, Head Infra Development & ESG, PISPL.
● Mr. Sanket Urkande, General Manager, GM Modular.
● Mr. Sidheshwar Tiwari, Senior Manager, HPCL.
● Mr. Visat Patel, Founder and CTO, Ushwa.

The panel discussion was moderated by Mr. Lalit Popli, senior managing partner, Green Edge Capital Advisors Pvt Ltd.

The panelists started the discussion by sharing their view on IoT technology and how it helps in tracking logistics. They emphasized that technology will not take their jobs but will create new opportunities for tech based roles and encouraged them to track changes in the environment.

Mr. Urkande also highlighted that technology will be affordable in the future for which he gave Tesla example. Mr. Patel explained the types of technologies that will be there in Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0 and said that Industry 4.0 will have technologies like AI, ML, AR, VR, etc, and Industry 5.0 will just be the enhancement of technologies that are already there.

Mr. Tiwari helped the participants understand how the government sector has started adopting and adapted technology lately and he also used his example of how HPCL uses AI and IoT sensors for reducing errors during the refining of crude. He also highlighted risks with the adoption of technology like lack of training and development of the operators, cyberattacks, etc. They also talked about sustainability and reducing carbon footprints by improving technologies in Industry 5.0.

The common theme that could be identified based on the panel discussion was the versatility of Industry 5.0 in different industries and the importance of training for developing new skill sets to support. The speakers encouraged students to reflect on the changing job roles and skill required to be better prepared.

Mr. Lalit Popli played a key role in navigating the discussions and engaging the audience as the moderator.

The panel discussion was divided into 3 segments

  1. Sector and Industry Perspective
  2. Company perspective
  3. Thematic discussion

Some of the key takeaways are

Sector and Industry Perspective

Data is really important in today's world. Companies rely a lot on data to make decisions and understand transaction behaviour through numbers. Data plays an important part in marketing and in understanding the company’s needs. Companies are realizing customer importance and are spending a lot on data based on customer spending and buying behaviour. People are becoming more and more conscious regarding the environment because of the content they are consuming which is creating more awareness related to their planet. The two types of sustainability were also highlighted, the first one being eco sustainability which talks about how companies can protect the environment and business sustainability which talks about how companies have to survive by focusing on generating profits.

Company’s Perspective

Many companies are redefining marketing strategies to adopt AI in their day-to-day business process. AI adoption in companies will lead to unassisted business growth in many business segments of companies. Brand storytelling plays an important role for companies to connect with customers. For example, for many people, the first house is an emotion and when they take a loan from HDFC, it shows their trust in the HDFC brand for this emotion.

Thematic Discussion

Technology is at a constant stage of evolution and marketers need to be updated regarding the same and its implementation in their respective fields. Metaverse is a very revolutionary concept and it will cause many disruptions. But it is still very far fetched as there is no proper infrastructure or ecosystem for the users like no good internet speed and connectivity. If a proper ecosystem is created for the Metaverse many people will adopt the concept and will provide significant revenue to the companies. Measuring the ROI of marketing innovation is crucial. For companies to understand and increase their ROI it is very important for them to understand their KPIs and increase their team productivity.

HROpSys 2022

Chetana's HR, Operations, and Systems students forum, HROpSys 2022, 'Disruptive Innovation in Business’ was held on 26th November 2022.

The theme was conceptualised considering the changing environment in the past few years and enabling students to understand trends and emerging best practices in Digital Transformation in HR and optimizing operations through a systems approach in organizations.

To gain deeper understanding on disruptive innovation in practice, it is important to look at real-life examples of disruptive technologies and business models that have transformed and re-imagined HR practices, re-defined operations and re-configured technology during the past decades.

The HROpSys 2022 was an endeavour to bring different corporate thought leaders to share their experiences of innovating and developing new business models for sustainability and growth. The speakers would focus on areas such as Digital Transformation through technology, optimizing operations, and contemporary strategic HR practices to put the strategy into action.

HROpSys 2022, launched the first Business Hackathon Competition at Chetana’s. The Business Hackathon was aimed at encouraging students to research and develop innovative ideas to deal with real-life issues faced by Organisations through integration of HR, Operations and Systems.

Based on the theme of the HROpSys, Disruptive Innovation in Business the following themes were shortlisted :

  • Disruptive Digital Transformation in business
  • Employee Health and safety
  • Leveraging technology for Employee Experience
  • Operational Innovation in rural areas
  • Service Innovation
  • Digital Security and Identity Management
  • Relentless Improvement an essential element for Business Growth

Based on the above theme students were required to identify a business problem specific to a sector / industry or an organization.

Business Hackathon 2022 had 11 teams comprising of students from HR, Operations and Systems (PGDM and MMS) were formed to ensure cross functional teams. Each team was required to select a theme, and then a sector / industry of their choice to research. The internal elimination round was judged by an expert panel of cross functional faculty judges. The top 4 finalist were judged by a panel of external judges comprising of alumni from CRKIMR and CIMR today after the panel discussion.

Students developed a detailed presentation demonstrating originality and uniqueness in their approach. The winning team was evaluated based on their Hackathon Pitch that was able to showcase the most feasible/creative solution to deal with real problems / issues and not complicated but practical and have the potential to be implemented. The solution had to incorporate dimensions of managing HR, optimizing Operations and adopting a Systems approach using technology.

Dr. Ramakrishnan, Chief Guest and Mr. Kedia, Key note speaker joined Dr. Madumita Patil, CEO, CRKIMR and CIMR, Dr. Kalim Khan, Director, CRKIMR, Dr. Nandita Mishra, Director, CIMR and Faculty of the HROpSys forum in lighting the lamp to commence the event.

Trends in Compensation and Benefits driving performance

Chetana’s HR Conclave 2020 ‘Bandhan …. Creating bonds’ in association with VANS Skilling and Advisory was held on 25th January, 2020. The theme for this year was ‘Trends in Compensation and Benefits Driving Performance’, which was inspired by the need for organisations to align their HR strategies and practices with industry 4.0, which has led to the growing importance of Smart Human Resources 4.0 (Smart HR 4.0). There is a need for leaders to adopt a forward-thinking in their HR strategy and practices and design compensation and benefits programs that can attract and retain the right talent and combat talent management challenges.

The forum brought inspirational business leaders and HR professionals to share their experience and best practices on Compensation and Benefits strategies to drive performance and enhance the employee value proposition. Eminent HR leaders, practicing professionals, academicians, and students attended the event. The deliberations at the conclave enabled participants to gain insights on practical solutions and strategies that they could implement to develop a competitive advantage to today’s talent marketplace using compensation and rewards.

The event commenced with the lighting of the lamp by the Shri Shridatta Haldankar, President Chetana’s Trust, Dr. Madhumita Patil, CEO, and I/C Director CIMR, Dr. Jayashri Bhakay, Director, CRKIMR, keynote speaker Mr. Anubhav Gupta, Director with Deloitte’s Human Capital Consulting Practice and speakers for the day.

Keynote speaker, Mr. Anubhav Gupta gave significant industry insights about strategic incentives and executive compensation. He also spoke about the need for environmental governance. He inspired the HR aspirants to explore more opportunities in the domain and emphasised on the importance of compensation in any organisation.

The address was followed by a panel discussion on ‘Using compensation & benefits strategy to drive Employee Value Proposition’. The panel consisted of Ms. Shruti Shrivastava, Head of total rewards and HR service excellence at Kotak life, Ms. Vishakha Sawlapurkar ,Cluster Lead for Mercer, Ms. Shilpa Srivastava, HR Head of Allianz global corporate & specialty and Mr. Debashish Majumdar, HR Manager,Performance and reward at Marico Ltd. the moderator for this discussion was Dr. Mahesh Luthia Associate Professor and Area Chair (HR), CIMR.

The introduction of the panellists highlighted the considerable experience and expertise of each speaker in compensation and benefits. In his opening remarks as the moderator, Dr. Mahesh Luthia opened the discussion by asking the panellists to share their experiences on how organizations can create a fascinating employee value proposition in a diverse workforce and how does it get manifested in the whole compensation agenda. The panel discussion revolved around various key areas related to different approaches and challenges around designing a holistic compensation strategy that would help organisations attract and retain talent in the current dynamic environment.

Ms. Shruti Srivastava spoke on how organizations can create a fascinating employee value proposition by adopting a philosophy "Careers for life" where employees can experience opportunities for growth within the organisation. She shared her practical experience on how HR can provide a compelling value proposition to attract young talent and retain the diverse workforce. She encourage students that they must focus on building a strong knowledge-based before they take up a career in the industry.

Ms. Vishakha Sawlapurkar shared her views on how the employee value proposition can motivate employees and the need to identify personas of employees to customize rewards. She also outlined the importance of developing an emotional connect with employees while adopting a total rewards approach in compensation. She shared her practical perspective on how employee engagement can contribute to this process or retaining key talent. While addressing the question on the challenges firms face to address the diverse needs of the current workforce, she explained the role of a strong organisation culture and the importance of aligning performance to pay.

Mr. Debashish Majumdar highlighted the importance of creating employee experiences as a value proposition. He highlighted how organisations can focus on employee experiences while administering compensation and benefits and how these benefits are relevant to the diverse new-age workforce. He also explained how firms are aligning their compensation strategy to meet expectations of the younger workforce joining organisations with technology capabilities and need for faster growth.

Ms. Shilpa Srivastava addressed the question on how compensation and rewards strategy are maintaining a balance between using competitive compensation structures to retain talent within the organisation and managing costs while using outsourcing strategy. She mentioned how firms manage a balance between these two continuums. She emphasised that firms are constantly evaluating on which functions or tasks can be outsourced to achieve efficiency. Core functions cannot be outsourced and hence firms continue to attract, retain and reward talent.

Before concluding the panel discussion each panellist gave valuable advice to students and young budding HR professionals on the need to keep pace with change, learn through experience, develop networks and most important have a clear sense of purpose and values that would help them succeed in the long run. The participants had an opportunity to interact with the panellist during the Q & A session.

Convocation 2019

The MMS and PGDM batch, 2016- 18 of Chetana’s Institute of Management & Research had a magnificent convocation ceremony on 16 Feb 2019, at CIMR auditorium.

The Chief Guest, Mr. Hemant Songadkar, CGM - NABARD congratulated the students for their accomplishments and encouraged them to stay focused on carrying forward Chetana’s legacy of ethical leadership. He also highlighted how success is the culmination of hard work, perseverance and competence.

Chetana’s CEO, Dr. Madhumita Patil proudly presented the Annual Report of 2018-19, mentioning the different milestones in the institutes’ journey towards excellence and administered the oath.

The Directors conferred the graduation degrees/diplomas upon a total of 101 MMS, 176 PGDM and 59 PGDM-Marketing students and the meritorious students were felicitated with medals. The presidential award, in memory of the Founder President Late Shri Madhukarrao Chaudhari, for best academic performance, was received by Ms. Amruta Desai in MMS and Mr. Anvay Kuvalekar in PGDM, who sent a clear message to all that the future certainly belongs to those who believe in their dreams and turn it into reality through relentless efforts.
